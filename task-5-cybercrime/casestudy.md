## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime in India where scammers 
trick people into sending money or revealing sensitive information.

In a typical case, a college student named Aman receives a call from someone pretending 
to be a bank official. The caller says there is a problem with his account and asks him to verify his details.
The student is then sent a link or QR code and is told to approve a request on his UPI app to “receive money.” Without 
understanding, he enters his UPI PIN and approves the request. Instead of receiving money, the amount gets deducted from his account.

This fraud usually happens in steps. 
First, the scammer contacts the victim through a call, message, or social media. 
Second, they create urgency or fear, such as claiming the account will be blocked. 
Third, they ask the victim to click a link, scan a QR code, or enter their UPI PIN. 
Fourth, the victim unknowingly approves a payment request. 
Finally, the money is transferred instantly to the scammer.

Students, elderly people, and those new to digital payments are common targets. The consequences include financial loss, 
stress, and difficulty recovering the money. This shows the importance of awareness and careful online behavior.
